Overview

Analogyc keyboards (analog/continuous switches or specialized layouts) allow smoother control for certain osu! playstyles where subtle actuation and hybrid inputs can improve streaming and alternating patterns. This page covers recommended actuation, debounce, and bindings.

Recommended Settings

  • Polling rate: 1000 Hz if supported for lowest latency.
  • Debounce: Keep debounce low (2-5 ms) if your firmware exposes it; avoid chatter.
  • Actuation: For analog switches use a mid-range actuation to avoid accidental presses while preserving control.

Keybinds & Technique

Common osu! binds for analogyc setups are similar to keyboard play: index/middle alternating, with some players using analog input to perform micro-adjustments. Try bind combinations and practice timing on lower-BPM maps before applying to higher BPM streams.

Maintenance & Tips

Keep firmware updated, remap easily with QMK/VIA-compatible boards, and always test in training maps to validate your settings.
